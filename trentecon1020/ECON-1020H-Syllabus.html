Description:

An introductory study of the aggregate economy in terms of output, employment, and inflation using simple economic models. The course examines both economic trends and fluctuations, as well as macroeconomic policies. Selected aspects of financial markets, international trade, the balance of payments, and exchange rates are also included.

This is a lecture-based course, with additional tutorials, introducing the basic concepts of macroeconomics. The course focuses on three issues: long-run growth, the business cycle, and fiscal and monetary policies. To understand these issues, simple models for the aggregate economy are developed, using which the effects of fiscal and monetary policies are assessed. Along the course, recent and long-run Canadian experiences are used to illustrate theory. As these fundamentals are discussed, the course explores important questions in economics: What is the engine of economic growth? Why does aggregate output fluctuate? What is the appropriate role of government in a modern economy? Can financial crisis and deep recessions be prevented?

Learning Outcomes:

After completing the course, students should expect to have a good understanding and knowledge of the aggregate economy, and be able to use macroeconomic models to analyze aggregate economic phenomena in Canadian context.
